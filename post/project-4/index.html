Analyzing the Effect of Surfactants on Cloud Formation Activity

November 13, 2020

This was a project I completed during my research internship at Brookhaven National Lab, for the Regeneron STS competition. It was done in collaboration with Dr. Laura Fierce, and involved a mix of environmental science and data analysis aspects. In short, I wrote python scripts to determine what effect surfactants have on the formation of cloud nuclei, as aerosol-cloud interactions are currently one of the least understood aspects of climate modeling. Data was visualized usinng Matplotlib. In this project, I found:

  • The larger the dry diameter (size of the aerosol particle), the lower the critical supersaturation (peak of the curve) tends to be (first image)
  • Surfactants generally increase cloud formation activity, especially at lower environmental supersaturations (second image)

This project was one of my first real coding experiences, and I was glad that I was able to learn a lot about python and analyzing data.
